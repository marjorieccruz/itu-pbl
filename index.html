Understanding

What is Project-Based Learning?

Project-Based Learning (PBL) is a teaching method in which students learn by actively engaging in real-world and personally meaningful projects. Students work on a project over an extended period of time that engages them in solving a real-world problem or answering a complex question.

Unlike traditional instruction, PBL puts students at the center of the learning process. They demonstrate their knowledge and skills by creating a public product or presentation for a real audience, developing deep content knowledge as well as critical thinking, creativity, and communication skills.

Learning is not the product of teaching. Learning is the product of the activity of learners.

— John Holt, Educator

Foundation

The Seven Essential Elements

01

Challenging Problem

A meaningful problem or question that provides purpose and challenge, appropriate to students' level of knowledge and skill.

02

Sustained Inquiry

An extended, rigorous process of asking questions, finding resources, and applying information to develop answers and solutions.

03

Authenticity

Real-world context, tasks and tools, quality standards, or impact on personal concerns, interests, and issues in students' lives.

04

Student Voice & Choice

Students make decisions about the project, including how they work and what they create, with appropriate guidance from teachers.

05

Reflection

Students and teachers reflect on learning, the effectiveness of inquiry, project activities, quality of work, and obstacles encountered.

06

Critique & Revision

Students give, receive, and use feedback to improve their process and products, following structured protocols and rubrics.

07

Public Product

Students make their project work public by explaining, displaying, or presenting it to people beyond the classroom.

Implementation

The PBL Process

01

Launch the Project

Introduce the driving question and engage students with an entry event that sparks curiosity and interest.

02

Build Knowledge

Students learn content and skills needed for the project through mini-lessons, research, and exploration.

03

Develop & Critique

Teams create prototypes, draft solutions, and engage in structured feedback sessions to improve their work.

04

Present & Reflect

Share final products with an authentic audience and reflect on the learning journey and outcomes.

Impact

Why PBL Works

  • Deeper Learning
    Students develop mastery of academic content through application and practice in authentic contexts.
  • 21st Century Skills
    Builds critical thinking, collaboration, communication, and creativity essential for future success.
  • Increased Engagement
    Students are motivated by meaningful work that connects to their interests and real-world issues.
  • Equity & Access
    Provides opportunities for all students to succeed through multiple pathways and diverse strengths.
  • Career Preparation
    Mirrors workplace environments where collaboration and problem-solving are essential.
